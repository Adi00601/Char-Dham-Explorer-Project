Jagannath Temple

Temple Essentials



The Jagannath Temple is located in Puri, Odisha, India. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus

The temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of the Hindu god Vishnu, along with his siblings Balabhadra and Subhadra.

The annual Ratha Yatra, or Chariot Festival, of Jagannath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu festivals in India. During this festival, the deities are taken out of the temple in grand processions on elaborately decorated chariots.

The temple is renowned for its Kalinga style of architecture, characterized by its towering spire (called 'vimana') and intricate stone carvings.

The temple has a rich history dating back to ancient times, with origins tracing back to at least the 12th century. It has undergone several renovations and expansions over the centuries.

Various rituals are performed daily at the temple, including the offering of food (known as 'Mahaprasad') to the deities and the observance of different festivals throughout the year.

Only Hindus are allowed to enter the main sanctum of the temple. Non-Hindus can visit certain parts of the temple complex but are not permitted inside the main shrine.

Adjacent to the temple is the Ananda Bazaar, where devotees can buy various items such as flowers, prasad, and souvenirs.

One of the notable features of the temple is the Nila Chakra, a large disc-shaped wheel mounted atop the temple's spire, which is made of alloy and is considered sacred.

The temple is home to one of the largest kitchens in the world, known as the 'Mahaprasad' kitchen. Here, food is prepared for the deities and the thousands of devotees who visit the temple daily.

Before the Ratha Yatra, the deities undergo a ceremonial bath during the Snana Yatra. The idols are bathed with 108 pitchers of water mixed with sandalwood paste, perfumes, and herbs.

Lord Vishnu's Legacy


Lord Vishnu is worshipped in the form of Lord Jagannath at the Jagannath Temple. Along with Lord Jagannath, the temple also enshrines Lord Balabhadra (an incarnation of Lord Vishnu's elder brother, Balarama) and Devi Subhadra.

Lord Jagannath, often referred to as the "Lord of the Universe," is depicted as a dark-colored wooden deity with large round eyes and a smiling face. He holds a discus (chakra) and a conch shell (shankha) in two of his hands.

Lord Jagannath is considered an avatara (incarnation) of Lord Vishnu, specifically as an amalgamation of Lord Krishna and Lord Vishnu's other forms

Once every 12 or 19 years, the idols of Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Devi Subhadra are replaced in a ceremony known as Nabakalebara

The worship of Lord Jagannath at the Jagannath Temple is deeply rooted in Vaishnavism, one of the major traditions within Hinduism that venerates Lord Vishnu as the supreme deity.

While the Jagannath Temple is located in Puri, Odisha, its appeal transcends regional boundaries, attracting devotees from various parts of India and around the world.
